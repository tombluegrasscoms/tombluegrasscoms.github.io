WHAT IS VISION?

VISION is an elegant solution that gives body shops one-stop access to genuine discounted parts from vehicle manufacturers.

The ability to use these parts instead of those from other aftermarket suppliers can greatly reduce the cost of the job, driving total loss avoidance to keep more vehicles on the road, and bring in more trade for the repair centre.

For vehicle manufacturers, VISION represents a key channel through which they can make significant additional sales into the collision repair market, opening up a marketplace that has largely been closed to them.

In line with our drive to meet client needs with maximum versatility and efficiency, VISION has been devised to be scalable and flexible, and can be tailored to meet the requirements of each individual customer. As well as its day-to-day function of preparing rapid estimates for original manufacturer parts, it is also a powerful market intelligence tool that can drive flexible pricing policies and logistics procedures.

 

CASE STUDY
ENSURING COMPLIANCE

Region: UK

Sector: Software

   Problem:

Dealer X has an agreement with Body Shop A to act as an authorised repairer, the terms of which require the body shop to buy all genuine parts from the dealer. But how can the dealer be sure that the body shop is adhering to the terms of the agreement? If questioned, the body shop can state that they are 100 percent loyal. However, the dealer has no means of verifying this.

   Solution:

Install the VISION software in the body shop. Immediately this will provide analysis of recent estimates to ascertain if the dealer is getting 100 percent of the body shop’s business. Going forward, VISION allows the dealer to be proactive against the parts that are being purchased in the aftermarket.

   Outcome:

In one typical instance we compared an estimate from Body Shop A and an invoice covering parts for the vehicle received by Dealer X. The invoice totaled £374.98 and covered six part lines. However, the estimate had eight part lines. A quick comparison showed that a headlamp and front bumper had been purchased in the aftermarket. In those two parts alone the dealer had lost sales of £198.00: equating to a 35 percent increase in business that can be recovered using VISION.
